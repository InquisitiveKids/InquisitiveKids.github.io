Spring Concerts

Every May in Davis School there is a special concert. The Spring Concert celebrates the school year and students put all they have learned together during the year and there is a big show. All family members are welcomed-parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, siblings-anyone that is special to you.

Here is an intreview to a student from Davis School about the 2019 concert.

6
Hi, my name is Karen Jia and right now I am in second grade. I will tell you about last year's spring concert.....
Spring Concerts are usually based on a book, or story. Each first or second grade class in Davis is assigned a single part of the story-it's kind of like a musical. In music class, students will then vote on which character they wanted to be. After they vote on what character they wanted(my class then voted on the squirrel), it's time to brainstorm and bring it into action!
Me and my class chose which station of the three we wanted- Sound Affects, Acting, or Xylophones. All three of them are in different places of the Gym. I chose acting. Ms. Hannah first started by reading our part of the book. It got us brainstorming. After, she asked the class for what movements they think would be good for the big fat squirrel. In the book the squirrel was fat because it was eating acorn pie. So I thought of an idea of that might show that it was eating. Ross thought of another idea of it jumping from tree to tree and we also thought of it doing other climbing moves, like clawing its way up or something. That was our first rough draft. Then we moved onto xylophones. Our music teacher then helped us brainstorm what our pattern would be. I how it would match the movements we had created earlier. It had to be like a squirrel lightly hopping from tree to tree, climbing up and down. Finally Bryan came up with an fantastic idea. He suggested that we could do C D E F G shush, shush, G F E D C. It was perfect!
